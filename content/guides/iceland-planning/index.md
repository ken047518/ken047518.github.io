# Iceland Planning Guide - Everything You Need to Know

Iceland is one of the most unique destinations on Earth - a land of fire and ice where you can witness glaciers, volcanoes, geysers, and waterfalls all in one day. Here's everything you need to plan your Icelandic adventure.

## When to Visit Iceland

### Summer (June - August)

**Pros:**
- Midnight sun (nearly 24 hours of daylight)
- Warmest weather (10-15°C / 50-59°F)
- All roads accessible
- Best for hiking and camping
- Wildlife viewing (puffins!)

**Cons:**
- Most expensive season
- Very crowded
- Accommodations book up months in advance
- No Northern Lights

**Best for:** First-time visitors, hiking enthusiasts, families

### Winter (November - March)

**Pros:**
- Northern Lights visible
- Ice caves accessible
- Fewer tourists
- Lower prices
- Dramatic winter landscapes

**Cons:**
- Very short days (4-5 hours of daylight)
- Many roads closed
- Harsh weather conditions
- Some attractions inaccessible

**Best for:** Northern Lights chasers, photographers, budget travelers

### Shoulder Seasons (April-May, September-October)

**Pros:**
- Fewer crowds than summer
- Lower prices
- September-October: Northern Lights possible
- Decent weather
- Most roads still accessible

**Cons:**
- Unpredictable weather
- Some highland roads closed
- Shorter daylight hours

**Best for:** Budget-conscious travelers seeking balance

## How Long to Visit

**Minimum: 5-7 days**
- Ring Road basics
- Main attractions
- Golden Circle

**Ideal: 10-14 days**
- Complete Ring Road circuit
- Time for detours
- Weather flexibility
- Westfjords or highlands

**Extended: 2-3 weeks**
- Comprehensive exploration
- Westfjords
- Highlands
- Off-the-beaten-path locations

## Budget Planning

## Getting Around

### Renting a Car

**Requirements:**
- Valid driver's license for at least 1 year
- Minimum age: 20-25 (depends on car type)
- Credit card for deposit

**Types of Vehicles:**

**2WD/Small Car** ($50-80/day)
- Fine for Ring Road in summer
- Cannot go on F-roads
- Cheaper on gas
- Lower insurance costs

**4WD/SUV** ($80-150/day)
- Required for highlands (F-roads)
- Better in winter
- More space for luggage
- Essential for rough roads

**Camper Van** ($150-250/day)
- Accommodation + transport
- Save on hotels
- Camping flexibility
- Higher gas costs

### Insurance

**Mandatory:**
- CDW (Collision Damage Waiver) - usually included
- Theft Protection - usually included

**Highly Recommended:**
- SCDW/SAAP (Super CDW) - reduces deductible
- Gravel protection - covers windshield/undercarriage
- Sand and Ash Protection (SAAP) - for southern coast

**Check your credit card:** Some cards cover rental car insurance, but verify coverage in Iceland specifically.

### Driving Tips

**Rules:**
- Drive on the right
- Headlights always on
- Speed limits: 50 km/h urban, 80 km/h gravel, 90 km/h paved
- Never drink and drive (0.05% limit, strictly enforced)

**Safety:**
- Check road.is for conditions daily
- Check weather at vedur.is
- Never drive off-road (huge fines!)
- Single-lane bridges - slow down, watch for oncoming traffic
- Gravel roads - slow down before turns
- Sheep everywhere - drive carefully

**Gas Stations:**
- Not as frequent outside Reykjavik
- Fill up whenever you can
- Many are self-service with credit card
- N1 and Orkan are main chains

**Base Layer:**
- Thermal underwear
- Merino wool or synthetic
- Multiple pairs

**Mid Layer:**
- Fleece jacket
- Wool sweater
- Long-sleeve shirts

**Outer Layer:**
- Waterproof, windproof jacket
- Rain pants
- Insulated jacket (winter)

**Accessories:**
- Warm hat and gloves
- Buff/neck warmer
- Waterproof hiking boots
- Wool socks

- Phone charger and cables

## Food and Groceries

### Money-Saving Tips

**Shop at Bonus** - Cheapest supermarket (pink pig logo)
**Buy groceries in Reykjavik** - More selection, better prices
**Cook your own meals** - Most accommodations have kitchens
**Bring snacks** - Fewer options outside towns
**Tap water is free** - And delicious!

### Must-Try Foods

- Lamb soup (Kjötsúpa)
- Hot dogs (Pylsur) - try one at Bæjarins Beztu
- Skyr (Icelandic yogurt)
- Plokkfiskur (fish stew)
- Fermented shark (if you dare!)
- Icelandic beer

### Restaurant Reality

Restaurants are expensive. A basic meal easily costs $25-40. Save dining out for special occasions and cook most meals.

## Northern Lights

### When: September to April

### Requirements:
- Dark skies (away from light pollution)
- Clear weather
- Solar activity (KP index 2+)

### Apps:
- My Aurora Forecast
- Vedur.is (weather)

### Tips:
- Be patient - it's not guaranteed
- Stay up late (10 PM - 2 AM peak)
- Dress warmly - you'll be outside for hours
- Let your eyes adjust (15-20 minutes)
- Camera settings: Manual, high ISO (1600-3200), wide aperture (f/2.8 or lower), 15-30 second exposure

**ATMs:** Available in all towns

- **Vedur.is** - Weather forecast
- **SafeTravel** - Register your travel plans
- **Gas.is** - Find gas stations, compare prices
- **My Aurora Forecast** - Northern Lights predictions
- **112 Iceland** - Emergency services app

## Safety Tips

1. **Weather Changes Fast** - Be prepared for anything
2. **Respect Nature** - Stay on marked paths
3. **Register Travel Plans** - Use SafeTravel.is
4. **Never Turn Back to Waves** - Sneaker waves kill tourists
5. **Don't Drive Tired** - Take breaks
6. **Emergency Number:** 112
7. **Get Insurance** - Medical and car insurance
8. **Respect Private Property** - Don't trespass

## Sustainable Travel

- Stay on marked trails
- Don't disturb wildlife
- Take all trash with you
- Don't take rocks or shells
- Use designated campsites only
- Don't drive off-road (illegal and damages environment)
- Respect private property

## Final Tips

1. **Book accommodation early** (especially summer)
2. **Don't overschedule** - distances are deceptive
3. **Build in weather days** - conditions can close roads
4. **Bring reusable water bottle** - tap water is excellent
5. **Download offline maps** - cell service is spotty
6. **Be flexible** - Some of the best experiences are unplanned
7. **Start with full tank of gas** - Stations can be far apart
8. **Check your car thoroughly** - Document any damage before leaving rental lot

Iceland is expensive, weather-dependent, and requires planning - but it's absolutely worth it. The raw beauty, otherworldly landscapes, and unique experiences make it an unforgettable destination.
